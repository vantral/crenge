In Tiriyó, spatial relations are expressed by means of postpositions. This is characteristic of all Cariban languages (Meira 2009, p. 317). All of them have preserved the structure of this postpositional system to a different degree. In this system, each postposition can be segmented into the locational and directional exponents. As Table 1 below shows, Goal meaning is encoded in the -ka(na) segment while -o(ё) is for the locational meaning of PLACE. Topological specifications are encoded by one and the same inventory of postpositional stems in both Goal and Source situations.

GOAL PLACE SOURCE
IN ta-ka; tao; po-na ta-o, aw-ё, po pëe
ON reh-kii, juuwё, ta-ka juuw-ё, po, reht-ё
SUBSTANCE hta-ka, hka-ka hta-o, hka-o
APUD ranme ranme, ekata-o, ekunn-ё, po, rena-o
HUMAN ja
UNDER epinё epinё
GENERAL LOCATION po-na po

Table 1. Adnominal spatial marking in Tiriyó

At the same time, for Source marking, one does not choose between different postpositional stems depending on the topological configuration. Thus, in Tiriyó, Goal and Source demonstrate radical asymmetry as to the availability of the topological specification. Goal may be as topologically specified as Place while Source makes no topological distinctions.
