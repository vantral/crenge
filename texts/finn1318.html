In the Finnish language, spatial relationships are encoded using spatial cases and adpositions. Postpositions predominate, but prepositions are also used. Postpositions typically govern a noun in the genitive case, while prepositions govern a noun in the accusative case. In Finnish tradition, the illative, inessive, and elative cases are referred to as the internal series of cases, while the allative, adessive, and ablative cases are referred to as the external series.

In orientations of GOAL, PLACE, and SOURCE, the spatial system is fully symmetric. Affix indicators for encoding IN-localization include indicators of internal cases: illative (Goal), inessive (Place), elative (Source); adpositions sisälle, sisällä, sisältä respectively.

Similarly, for ON-localization, indicators of allative (Goal), adessive (Place), ablative (Source) or adpositions päälle, päältä, päällä respectively are used.

The encoding of nouns with the meaning of General Location varies between the internal and external series of cases depending on the noun's meaning. For GL+GOAL, the illative case indicator is primarily used. For some toponyms and events, the allative case indicator is used.

Likewise, the allative is used for some nouns, for example, 'station', 'platform', 'dump'.

For GL+PLACE, the inessive case is more commonly used. The adessive is used with demonstrative pronouns, sometimes with nouns.

The adessive also occurs with abstract entities.

With nouns denoting spaces (house, kitchen, shop), the inessive is used; with nouns where the surface is a default topological element, the adessive is used.

When encoding GL+SOURCE, the ablative is more commonly used with nouns denoting events and abstract entities, as well as with nouns denoting large surfaces.

The elative is used with nouns denoting spaces and events, primarily expressed by nominalizations, as well as with other nouns acting as General Location.

For encoding UNDER-localization in all directions, a combination of a noun and the postposition al- with an external indicator of the corresponding orientation is used: alle (Goal), alla (Place), alta (Source).

Localization APUD is encoded by a variety of adpositions containing a base meaning 'near' and indicators of locative cases, and in the orientation of PLACE sometimes by the adessive.
