Source-Goal Asymmetry in the Languages of the World

Investigating the distinction of topological configurations in 50 languages.

Project Overview

The study aims to investigate this asymmetry across 50 diverse languages by analyzing adnominal encoding of goals and sources in basic topological relations.

Data collection poses a methodological challenge as directional and topological meanings are rarely exhaustively covered in grammatical descriptions, requiring a thorough examination of textual examples for a fuller understanding of the language's spatial system.

Primary Values Tested
  • IN: Being inside a container
  • ON: Being on a flat horizontal surface
  • UNDER: Being under a reference point without touching it
  • APUD: Being in close proximity to a reference point without further specification

Research Methods

We plan to gather a database of spatial relationship expression strategies from 50 languages. Data will be sourced from the most comprehensive grammatical descriptions or annotated corpora available for each language.
